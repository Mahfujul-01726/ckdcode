Chronic Kidney Disease Prediction

A Comparative Study Using Machine Learning on Patient Clinical Records
Based on Hassan et al. (2023), Human-Centric Intelligent Systems

Research Implementation & Analysis

The Problem: CKD Crisis

  • 37 million Americans suffer from Chronic Kidney Disease (15% of adult population)
  • 90% of patients are unaware of their condition
  • CKD is called the "Silent Assassin" - no obvious early signs
  • Early detection minimizes disease progression and treatment costs
  • Accurate prediction models essential for healthcare intervention
Our Goal: Develop the most accurate ML-based CKD prediction system using clinical patient data

Related Work & Context

Existing CKD Prediction Approaches:

  • Almansour et al. [26]: ANN & SVM - 99.75% accuracy
  • Khan et al. [12]: Multiple classifiers - 98.25% with SVM
  • Gunarathne et al. [14]: Multiclass Decision Forest - 99.1%
  • Abdullah et al. [16]: RF with feature selection - 98.825%
Our Innovation: XGBoost-based feature selection with SHAP values + 5 ML algorithms on dual datasets

Research Pipeline

  • 1. Data Loading & Exploration: 400 patient records, 25 clinical attributes
  • 2. Preprocessing: Handle missing values with Predictive Mean Matching (PMM)
  • 3. K-Means Clustering: Identify patient subgroups (k=3)
  • 4. Feature Selection: XGBoost with SHAP analysis → 7 key features
  • 5. Dataset Splitting: 80% train / 20% test (stratified)
  • 6. Model Training: NN, RF, SVM, RT, BTM on both datasets
  • 7. Evaluation: Accuracy, Sensitivity, Specificity, Kappa metrics

ML Algorithms Applied

  • Neural Network (NN): 2 hidden layers (64, 32 neurons)
  • Random Forest (RF): 100 decision trees, ensemble voting
  • Support Vector Machine (SVM): RBF kernel, C=10
  • Random Tree (RT): ExtraTreesClassifier, random splits
  • Bagging Tree Model (BTM): 100 bagged decision trees
Evaluation Strategy: All models tested on both 25-feature and 7-feature (XGBoost) datasets

Performance Analysis

Why These Metrics Matter:

  • Accuracy: Overall correctness - essential for reliable diagnosis
  • Sensitivity (Recall): Detects CKD patients - minimize false negatives
  • Specificity: Correctly identifies non-CKD patients - minimize false alarms
  • Kappa (Cohen's): Inter-rater agreement - accounts for chance agreement
Clinical Significance: SVM on 7-feature set offers perfect performance with minimal computation
